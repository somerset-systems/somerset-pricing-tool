# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Salespeople at Somerset Systems. They use this tool before a client call: filling it out in preparation, then screensharing the output during a Zoom with a prospect. Prospects are typically owners or operators of HVAC contractors, electrical contractors, or legal firms — small-to-mid-size businesses evaluating a first automation engagement. The primary task on any given screen is configuring or reviewing a pricing and ROI output, quickly and confidently, in front of a live client.

## Product Purpose

A pre-call pricing and ROI calculator that generates a transparent, client-facing Operational Opportunity Assessment. It exists so Somerset salespeople can walk prospects through a credible, number-backed proposal during a live Zoom call. Success looks like a prospect who understands exactly how Somerset arrived at the price and why the ROI calculation holds — and trusts it.

## Brand Personality

Premium, precise, authoritative. The tool should feel like a well-prepared senior consultant, never a software product, never a spreadsheet. Somerset positions itself above typical software consultancies; the interface should reflect that.

## Anti-references

Generic Excel/PDF-style reports: plain, unstyled tables and outputs with no visual hierarchy, no brand presence, nothing that differentiates the work from a commodity estimate. The output panel in particular must not look like something anyone could generate in a spreadsheet.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through transparency.** Every number the tool produces must show its formula or calculation trace. No black-box outputs — the client must be able to follow the math.
2. **Presentation-grade output.** The Step 4 output panel is designed for screensharing during a Zoom call. Hierarchy, legibility, and visual weight are calibrated for a 13–15" screen viewed by two people, not a single reader at a desk.
3. **Expert confidence.** The interface should feel like a senior consultant prepared this document, not a form wizard a salesperson filled out. Restraint in decoration, weight in substance.
4. **Signal over noise.** Prioritize readability and hierarchy over completeness. Each section earns its place. Visual clutter is a trust problem.
5. **Brand coherence.** This tool is Somerset on-screen. Every design choice reinforces premium, precise positioning.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal compliance target. Aim for high contrast and keyboard usability as a matter of craft, not compliance. No specific assistive technology requirements.
